Japanese food!

By Helen

Traditional foods

Ramen

Ramen is a bowl of noodles covered in a pork, beef or miso broth, often with toppings such as meat, seaweed or an egg

Sushi

Sushi can come in many shapes but it usually has the same or similar ingredients. (rice, protein such as fish and seaweed)

Mochi

Mochi is a flavoured ball of sweet glutinous rice, sometimes with filling inside it. It comes in many flavours such as green tea, strawberry and red bean. You can get it with ice cream as the filling.

Staple Ingredients.

Rice

Rice is an essential ingredient in many japanese foods such as Sushi, Mochi, Donbouri, Bento and many others.

Seaweed

Seaweed is sometimes used as a decoration or as a wrapper on things like sushi.

Noodles. They are in many dishes and there are many types such as Soba, Udon and Ramen

Fish

Because Japan is surrounded by water, there are many fishing towns.

Daily Menu

Breakfast

Traditional Japanese breakfasts usually include Miso soup, Rice, Grilled fish and pickled vegetables, however, most of the time they usually just eat a normal western breakfast (Cereal,Toast.)

Lunch

Often, Japanese people go out to get street food like Takoyaki or go to convenience stores to buy Prepackaged food like sandwiches and Bento boxes, Bento boxes often include meat, rice and vegetables.

Dinner

In Japan, they often serve all of the meals up at once. It usually consists of a main meal and side dishes. Often side dishes include pickled vegetables, Rice and Soup.

Dessert

Japanese desserts include Mochi, Dango and Dorayaki which is a type of sponge cake with red bean paste inside of it.

Japanese cooking methods

Japanese cooking methods include:

Agemono which is frying. Common fried foods include Tempura and Tonkatsu

Mushimono which is steaming. Common Steamed foods include Chawanmushi which is a steamed egg dish

Nimono which is boiling. Common boiled foods include Sukiyaki which is similar to chinese hotpot

Yakimono which is grilling. Common grilled foods include Yakitori which is a chicken skewer.

Eating foods raw (I couldn't find the japanese word for it). Common raw foods include sashimi and sushi

Influence on NZ

There are many Sushi shops around New Zealand and many more Japanese resturants. There are plenty of different japanese snacks in supermarkets. in addition to that there are many asian supermarkets that have japanese goods.
